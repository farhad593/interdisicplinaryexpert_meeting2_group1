Olafur Eliasson

Installation Art & Environmental Works

About

Olafur Eliasson is a Danish-Icelandic artist known for his large-scale installations and sculptures that employ elemental materials such as light, water, and air temperature to enhance the viewer's experience. His work often addresses climate change, sustainability, and perception.

Education

  • Royal Danish Academy of Fine Arts, Copenhagen
  • Founder, Studio Olafur Eliasson

Selected Exhibitions

  • 2023 - "Olafur Eliasson: Life" - Fondation Beyeler, Basel
  • 2019 - "In Real Life" - Tate Modern, London
  • 2016 - "Versailles" - Palace of Versailles, France

Selected Works

The Weather Project

Installation with monofrequency lights, projection foil, haze machines, mirror foil, 2003

Monumental artificial sun installation at Tate Modern's Turbine Hall.

New York City Waterfalls

Public art installation, 2008

Four artificial waterfalls installed along the East River in New York City.

Your Rainbow Panorama

Colored glass walkway installation, 2011

Circular, rainbow-colored glass walkway on the roof of ARoS Art Museum.

Ice Watch

Installation with Greenland ice blocks, 2014

Installation of melting icebergs highlighting climate change.

Little Sun

Solar-powered LED lamp, 2012

Global project providing clean, affordable light to communities without electricity.

Din Blinde Passager

Tunnel installation with fog and colored lights, 2010

21-meter-long tunnel filled with dense fog and colored lights.
