Jenny Saville

Contemporary Figure Painting

About

Jenny Saville is a British contemporary painter and member of the Young British Artists movement. Known for her large-scale paintings of the female figure, her work explores themes of flesh, form, and identity through a distinctive and powerful painterly style.

Education

  • BA, Glasgow School of Art
  • Honorary Degree, University of Oxford

Selected Exhibitions

  • 2023 - "Jenny Saville: New Paintings" - Gagosian Gallery, New York
  • 2022 - "Jenny Saville: Continuous Line" - Scottish National Gallery of Modern Art
  • 2021 - "Jenny Saville: Elpis" - Museo Novecento, Florence

Selected Works

Propped

Monumental female nude examining themes of female representation and self-image.

Plan

Large-scale nude with surgical markings exploring body modification and medical intervention.

Fulcrum

Oil on canvas, 1999

Massive triptych depicting intertwined bodies, examining mass and flesh.

Reflective Flesh

Oil on canvas, 2002

Study of flesh and reflection exploring the materiality of the human body.

Red Stare Head IV

Oil on canvas, 2006-2011

Intense close-up portrait exploring facial features and expression.

The Mothers

Oil on canvas, 2011

Series exploring themes of motherhood, fertility, and the female body.
