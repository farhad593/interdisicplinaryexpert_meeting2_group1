Kara Walker

Contemporary Art & Silhouettes

About

Kara Walker is an American contemporary artist known for her exploration of race, gender, sexuality, violence, and identity in her work. She is best known for her room-size tableaux of black cut-paper silhouettes that examine the history of American race relations.

Education

  • Rhode Island School of Design, MFA
  • Atlanta College of Art, BFA

Selected Exhibitions

  • 2023 - "Kara Walker: Cut to the Quick" - Cincinnati Art Museum
  • 2019 - "Kara Walker: Fons Americanus" - Tate Modern, London
  • 2014 - "A Subtlety" - Domino Sugar Factory, Brooklyn

Selected Works

Gone: An Historical Romance of a Civil War as It Occurred Between the Dusky Thighs of One Young Negress and Her Heart

Cut paper installation, 1994

Room-sized installation of cut-paper silhouettes exploring themes of American racial history.

A Subtlety, or the Marvelous Sugar Baby

Sugar, polystyrene, installation, 2014

Monumental sugar-coated sphinx-like sculpture addressing the history of sugar trade.

Fons Americanus

Mixed media installation, 2019

13-meter tall working fountain exploring the interconnected histories of Africa, America, and Europe.

The End of Uncle Tom and the Grand Allegorical Tableau of Eva in Heaven

Cut paper installation, 1995

Large-scale silhouette installation examining racial stereotypes in literature.

After the Deluge

Mixed media installation, 2006

Installation exploring the aftermath of Hurricane Katrina through historical and contemporary lens.

Darkytown Rebellion

Cut paper and projection installation, 2001

Large-scale installation combining silhouettes with colored light projections.
