Ai Weiwei

Contemporary Conceptual Art

About

Ai Weiwei is a Chinese contemporary artist and activist known for his powerful installations and works addressing social justice and human rights. His practice spans architecture, curatorial work, photography, film, and social criticism.

Education

  • Parsons School of Design, New York
  • Art Students League of New York

Selected Exhibitions

  • 2023 - "Ai Weiwei: Making Sense" - Design Museum, London
  • 2021 - "Trace" - Skirball Cultural Center, Los Angeles
  • 2019 - "Ai Weiwei: Bare Life" - Kemper Art Museum, St. Louis

Selected Works

Sunflower Seeds

Porcelain, 2010

Installation of 100 million hand-painted porcelain sunflower seeds.

Dropping a Han Dynasty Urn

Photographic triptych, 1995

Performance documentation of destroying an ancient artifact.

Forever Bicycles

Installation with bicycles, 2011

Monumental installation made from 1,500 bicycles exploring mass production.

Straight

Steel rebar, 2008-2012

Installation of straightened rebar from schools destroyed in the Sichuan earthquake.

Remembering

Backpacks installation, 2009

Wall of backpacks commemorating students lost in the Sichuan earthquake.

Law of the Journey

Inflatable boat installation, 2017

Monumental installation addressing the global refugee crisis.
